Olukayode Esther | WDD 130

Hello, My name is Esther Olukayode, and I proudly hail from the Yoruba ethnic group in the Southwest region of Nigeria. I currently reside in Lagos State. As the first child in my family, I’ve grown up with a strong sense of responsibility, curiosity, and a deep appreciation for learning.

From a young age, I’ve always been drawn to books in my father's library and ideas that could challenge me to think differently. That love for knowledge has shaped much of who I am today. Whether I’m reading a novel, or engaging in thoughtful conversations, I find joy in anything that stretches my mind and helps me grow.

Driven by this passion for learning and a desire to solve real-world problems, I’m currently studying Web Development at BYU-Idaho. This journey into tech is more than just a course for me — it’s a step toward becoming a creator of solutions. I believe technology holds the power to transform lives, and I want to be part of that transformation by building tools and platforms that make everyday life better for people in my community and beyond.

Lagos, Nigeria

Lagos is a busy and lively city in Nigeria. It sits by the Atlantic Ocean and is full of energy, people, and movement. The city has tall buildings, crowded markets, and beautiful beaches. It’s known for music, fashion, and food. People of various tribes come to Lagos to work, study, or start businesses. Traffic can be heavy, but there’s always something happening. From street vendors to big companies, Lagos is full of life. It’s a mix of old and new, with history and modern style side by side. Lagos is a place of dreams, hustle, and endless possibilities.
